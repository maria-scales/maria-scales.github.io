Probability of a Career Page: The Math
Jun 2026 · Supporting analysis

The sourcing cycle in the main article assumes that visiting a company's careers page is a viable alternative to relying solely on LinkedIn. This note justifies the assumption: at least 74% of companies actively posting on LinkedIn have a dedicated careers page.

Two Predictive Triggers

The presence of a careers section on a corporate website can be predicted with high accuracy from two factors: the application method used and the company's size.

Trigger 1: Application Button Type

  • "Apply" button (redirects to external domain / ATS) → probability of a dedicated career page: ~99%
  • "Easy Apply" (CVs collected directly within LinkedIn) → probability: 60–80%, as early-stage startups without their own infrastructure frequently use this feature

Trigger 2: Company Size

  • Up to 10 employees — rarely have a career page: 30–50%
  • 11–50 employees — basic hiring automation typical: 70–85%
  • 50+ employees — industry standard: 95%+

Weighted Probability Calculation

The calculation uses the distribution of companies actively investing in job postings on LinkedIn — skewed toward more mature businesses, since micro-companies hire less frequently and in smaller volumes.

Conclusion

If you take a random company currently running an active job posting on LinkedIn, the probability that it has its own website with a careers section is approximately 74%.

If you exclude micro-businesses (1–10 employees) and "Easy Apply" postings from the sample, the probability exceeds 90%.

This makes direct career page monitoring a reliable input to the sourcing cycle — not a gamble.

Note: The firmographic probability estimates (30–50%, 70–85%, 95%+) are the author's own estimates based on general industry benchmarks and observed hiring patterns, not externally sourced figures. The weighted calculation reflects a reasonable approximation for illustrative purposes.
