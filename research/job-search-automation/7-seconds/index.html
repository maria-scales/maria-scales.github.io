The 7-Second Resume Screen: Evidence and Sources
Jun 2026 · Supporting analysis

The claim that recruiters spend ~7 seconds on initial CV review is frequently cited but rarely sourced. Below is a compilation of the primary study and supporting confirmations, ordered by evidence quality.

Primary Source: Eye-Tracking Study

The most compelling evidence comes from hardware-based research using eye-tracking technology to monitor recruiters' actual eye movements — rather than relying on self-reported survey responses.

  • TheLadders (2018) — Eye-tracking data showed an average initial screening time of 7.4 seconds. Article with breakdown and heatmaps · Full PDF report

Major Job Boards and Career Platforms

Platforms with large internal datasets independently confirm the 6–7 second range in their career guidance:

  • Indeed — The world's largest job site cites an average of 6 to 7 seconds in its career blog. How Long Do Employers Look at Resumes?
  • Zety — Aggregates surveys from HR professionals. HR Statistics
  • Novoresumé — Uses the "6-second rule" as the foundation for ATS-friendly template design. How to Write a CV

Academic and Institutional Confirmations

Leading universities and professional associations reference the same metric in official career guidance:

  • Harvard University (Office of Career Services) — Emphasises formatting for quick scanning and front-loading key information. Harvard Resume and Cover Letter Guide
  • SHRM (Society for Human Resource Management) — The world's largest HR professionals association; publications regularly reference the need to optimise for a few-second initial scan. Screening and Evaluating Job Candidates
